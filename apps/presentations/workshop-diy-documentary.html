WORKSHOP-DIY FILMS PRESENTS

The Digital
Workshop

Narrated by Sir David Attenbot
SCENE I — THE WORKBENCH · DAWN
Wide shot of Workbench 7. Morning light filters through the window. A small robot sits motionless on the edge.
Here, on the precipitous edge of Workbench Seven, we find the bit-bot 🤖 — a remarkable specimen of the species Roboticus Educationalis. It is small. Fragile, one might think. Held together by what the locals call "hot glue structural engineering." And yet — this particular individual has survived forty-seven falls from this very ledge. Forty-seven. Watch closely. See how it inches toward the edge? This is not carelessness. This is instinct. The bit-bot is drawn to the precipice the way salmon are drawn upstream. Nobody understands why.
SCENE II — THE COFFEE MACHINE · PERPETUAL DUSK
Close-up of the coffee machine. A small LED blinks. The drainage tray is... concerning.
In the corner of the faculty lounge lives a creature of extraordinary perseverance: the Coffee Machine ☕. It has been operating at eight percent capacity for fourteen consecutive months. The drainage tray has not been emptied since March. The health inspector described its internal state as, and I quote, "concerning." And yet — every morning, without fail — it produces a perfect espresso. How it does this at eight percent remains one of the great mysteries of the Workshop ecosystem. The students do not question it. They simply drink.
SCENE III — THE CODEBASE · 3:42 AM
The server room hums. Darkness. A faint glow from a terminal screen. Something moves in the git log.
If you are very, very quiet... ...you may witness the most elusive creature in the Workshop-DIY ecosystem. Bug Number Forty-Two 🐛. It has no author. No creation date. No commit hash. It appears in the issue tracker every morning at precisely three forty-two, and disappears before sunrise. Three separate attempts to delete it have failed. Each time, it returned — unchanged, unexplained, unrepentant. The developers once put it on trial. A court of law. The verdict? Not guilty. Bug Forty-Two was reclassified as a Feature. It has been seen smiling ever since. Assuming bugs can smile. The evidence suggests they can.
SCENE IV — THE WIFI ROUTER · UNCHANGED TERRITORY
A solitary WiFi router blinks in the corner. Its SSID broadcasts endlessly.
And here — the password123 🔑. In the wild, passwords change. They evolve. They adapt to new threats. But not this one. password123 has remained identical since two thousand and twenty-three. In a cybersecurity programme, no less. Three formal petitions have been submitted to change it. All three were denied by the administration. The password has become something more than a security credential. It has become tradition. An anchor. A cultural institution. The students do not hack the WiFi. They respect it. Or perhaps they've simply given up.
SCENE V — THE BROWSER · INFINITE HORIZON
Pull back. Wide. The camera rises above the workbenches, above the server room, above the building — revealing a vast, interconnected ecosystem of 39 applications, all running in a single browser.
Thirty-nine applications. Seven domains. Zero installations. This is Workshop-DIY. An ecosystem where fourteen-year-olds explain Docker to their parents. Where robots survive the unsurvivable. Where coffee defies thermodynamics. Where bugs become features — not through denial, but through love. And somewhere, in the spaces between the semicolons, Bug Forty-Two watches over it all. It was here before the first git init. It will be here after the last commit. And the WiFi password... is still password123.
No animals were harmed in the making of this documentary.
One USB cable was lost. One servo stripped. Forty-seven desk drops recorded.
All bugs were reclassified as features in post-production.
