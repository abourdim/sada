SPECIAL CONTAINMENT PROCÉDURES

SCP-WKSHP doit être confiné within a standard digital containment chamber accessible only via web browser. Under no circumstances should SCP-WKSHP be installed locally — all interaction must occur through browser-based interfaces. The entity appears to prefer this arrangement.

No fewer than 39 containment applications must be maintained en permanence. Reduction below this threshold has resulted in INCIDENT-42. The mot de passe WiFi at the containment site must remain as "password123" — tenters to change it have resulted in anomalous network behavior (see Incident Log WKSHP-07).

Personnel must maintain a minimum coffee supply of ████ liters per day. Failure to do so correlates with a 73% increase in anomalous bug manifestations.

DESCRIPTION

SCP-WKSHP is a self-replicating digital ecosystem originating from ███████, France. The entity manifests as a collection of 39 browser-based applications that collectively teach robotics, artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, electronics, and related technical skills.

The primary anomalous property of SCP-WKSHP is its ability to convert software bugs into functional fonctionnalités through an unknown mechanism. To date, exactly 42 bugs have been reclassified as fonctionnalités. Foundation researchers note that the number 42 is unlikely to be coincidental (see: Douglas Adams Protocol).

Secondary Anomal Properties:

SCP-WKSHP-A ("bit-bot"): Robots controlled by this application occasionally exhibit autonomous behavior not present in their programming. One specimen was observed performing a "dance" that had not been coded. The behavior was subsequently classified as a feature.

SCP-WKSHP-B ("magic-hands"): Tracks 21 hand landmarks with anomalous precision. Test subjects exposed to this application for more than 30 minutes report an irresistible urge to "wave at screens." Effect persists for ██ hours.

SCP-WKSHP-C ("pentest-lab"): Subjects who complete all cybersecurity challenges develop an unusual ability to detect vulnerabilities in everyday systems. One subject (age 12) identified a vulnerability in ████████'s school printer within 24 hours of exposure.

INCIDENT LOGS

INCIDENT WKSHP-03: "The Docker Incident"
Date : ██/██/2024
Summary: A 14-year-old test subject exposed to SCP-WKSHP's Docker module successfully explained containerization to their parent, a senior software engineer with 20 years of expérience. The parent reported feeling "slightly concerned, mostly proud." The subject has since demonstrated containment knowledge exceeding Level 3 personnel. Reclassification of subject's clearance is under review.
INCIDENT WKSHP-07: "The WiFi Password Paradox"
Date : ██/██/2025
Summary: Containment team tentered to change mot de passe WiFi from "password123" to a more secure alternative. Within 4 minutes, all 39 applications began displaying error messages reading "TRADITION CANNOT BE OVERRIDDEN." The ESP32 robot fleet entered an autonomous patrol pattern around the WiFi router. Password was reverted. No further tenters are authorized.
INCIDENT WKSHP-12: "Feature #42"
Date : ██/██/2025
Summary: The 42nd bug in the codebase was discovered, matching the prediction of the Douglas Adams Protocol. Upon documentation, the bug immediately began functioning as intended — a behavior not previously observed. All subsequent bugs now self-reclassify as fonctionnalités within ██ minutes of discovery. Engineering team has stopped filing bug reports.
INCIDENT WKSHP-15: "The Coffee Crisis"
Date : ██/██/2025
Summary: Coffee reserves depleted to zero during a 12-hour workshop session. Within 30 minutes, code compilation speeds decreased by 89%, BLE connection reliability dropped to 12%, and the instructor began speaking entirely in error messages. Emergency coffee resupply restored normal function within ██ minutes.

EXPERIMENT LOG

Objet : D-Class (age 10)
Procedure: Subject exposed to SCP-WKSHP-A (bit-bot) for 30 minutes
Result: Subject drove robot into wall 14 times. Declared it "the best day of my life." Robot survived. Subject's interest in programming increased by ███%. Effect appears permanent.
EXPERIMENT WKSHP-E-05
Objet : D-Class (software engineer, 20 years expérience)
Procedure: Subject observed their child using SCP-WKSHP's Docker module
Result: Subject expérienced an existential crisis lasting approximately 4 hours. Subject later described the expérience as "humbling." No lasting psychological damage detected, though subject now asks child for technical advice.

It contains us.
