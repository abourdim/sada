SUMMARY OF INCIDENT

Le 28 février 2026, vers 14:42, un câble USB-C (hereinafter "the deceased") a été trouvé non-responsive on Workbench 7 of the Workshop-DIY Robotique Lab. Despite multiple resuscitation attempts including reseating, switching orientations (which should work both ways — this is USB-C), and the application of compressed air, the cable failed to establish data or power connection.

The deceased was a third-party USB-C to USB-C cable, approximately 1 meter in length, white in color, with no visible external damage. It had been in active service for approximately 4 months, connecting ESP32 development boards to student laptops.

AUTOPSY RÉSULTATS

Upon internal examination using a continuity tester, the following was observed:

• VBUS line: OPEN (no continuity) — Power delivery compromised
• D+ / D- lines: INTERMITTENT — Data transmission unreliable
• CC line: SHORT TO GROUND — Configuration channel failure
• Shield: INTACT — External damage not a factor

Cause du décès is determined to be internal conductor fatigue resulting from repeated acute-angle stress events (bending), combined with at least three incidents of "yanking by the cable instead of the connector" (see witness statements).

WITNESS STATEMENTS

Student A (age 13):"I was just trying to flash the ESP32. I pulled the cable and it came out. I didn't pull hard. Okay, maybe a little hard. But the code was compiling and I was excited."
Student B (age 14):"I saw Student A yank the cable sideways while the laptop was on the edge of the desk. The cable made a sound. Not a good sound."
The ESP32 Board:"I felt the connection drop at 14:42:03. One moment VBUS was at 5V. The next, nothing. Silence. I've lost 7 cables this year. You get used to it. You never really get used to it."
bit-bot:"I was on Workbench 3. I didn't see anything. But I heard the snap. We all heard the snap."

CONTRIBUTING FACTORS

Investigation revealed the following systemic issues:

1. The cable was a €2.99 third-party purchase (insufficient quality for educational environment)
2. No strain relief was present at either connector end
3. The cable was routinely coiled at a radius below manufacturer recommendations
4. At least 3 separate students used the cable as a leash to prevent the ESP32 from "escaping"
5. The cable was once used to "tie" a micro:bit to a desk (unconfirmed)

RECOMMENDATIONS

1. Replace all third-party cables with certified USB-IF compliant units
2. Install "PULL THE CONNECTOR NOT THE CABLE" signage at all workbenches
3. Conduct a moment of silence for the deceased
4. Budget €50/month for cable replacement (based on historical mortality rate of 1.7 cables/week)
