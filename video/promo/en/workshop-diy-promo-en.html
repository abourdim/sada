The Hook — "What if kids could..."

Audio
Ambient hum → keyboard clicks → upbeat electronic music drops

Workshop-DIY logo reveal at end of sequence

The Problem — "Screen zombies"
0:15–0:30
Visual
Split screen: Left — kid passively scrolling on a phone, gray tones. Right — same kid building a robot, vibrant colors. The right side "breaks through" and takes over the full screen.
Voice
"Kids spend 7 hours a day on screens. What if those hours became superpowers?"
Music
Tension builds → resolves as the colorful side wins

The Workshop — "A lab in Chelles"
0:30–0:50
Visual
Aerial shot of Chelles → zoom into the workshop space. Kids (6–16) at tables: one programs a micro:bit, another builds a robot, another types code. Real footage feel — handheld camera, natural lighting.
Voice
"In Chelles, France, kids aged 6 to 16 don't just use technology — they build it. Welcome to Workshop-DIY."
Text
Lower third: "Workshop-DIY · Association Loi 1901 · Chelles 77500"

The Apps — "86 apps, zero installs"
0:50–1:05
Visual
Rapid-fire montage of app screenshots: CowSay Kids, Bit-Bot, Crypto Academy, SDR Kids Lab, Hacktivist Kids. Each appears for 1 second with a satisfying "pop" animation. Counter in corner ticks up: 1... 10... 39... 86.
Voice
"86 browser-based apps. Robots, AI, radio, cryptography, ethical hacking. Open a browser — that's it."
Text
"86 Apps · 7 Domains · 0 npm install"

The Hardware — "Real tools, real learning"
1:05–1:20
Visual
Close-up shots: micro:bit V2 lighting up LEDs, ESP32 connecting to WiFi, RTL-SDR dongle receiving FM radio, HackRF showing a spectrum display. Each device held by a kid's hands.
Voice
"From micro:bit to HackRF — real hardware, in real hands, doing real things."
Text
Device names and prices appear next to each: "micro:bit V2 · 15€" etc.

Quick comedic sequence: a kid discovers Bug #42 in the code — the bug "escapes" the screen (animated overlay), orders a pizza, changes the WiFi password to "password123" again. Coffee machine displays "8%".

Warm, emotional, genuine. Human moments.
